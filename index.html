Goodreads Redesign

We were given the opportunity to redesign the Goodreads website. We started our redesign endeavors by conducting surveys. These surveys asked questions such as, "what do you like about the website?", "if you could change anything, what would you change?", "could you navigate through the site easily", "do you enjoy the features of this site.", and so on.


Based on my initial answers, I began to research and plan out my redesign. Some initial challenges that I was faced with was...Goals of the redesign were to make the website more user friendly.

Colors

For colors I had the idea of a rustic coffee shop color palette. I wanted to incorporate browns, soft blues, and white. Based on feedback I received, users stated that the site seems "bland", or "visually lacking". So without going too drastic, I wanted to add in colors of delight.

Typography

Typeface was a challenge. I wanted to bring in fonts that would be appealing for young readers, but also not exclude traditional members of the site.
